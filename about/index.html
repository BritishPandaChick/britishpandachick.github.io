About Sarah

Sarah Bartley is a front-end web developer and content creator at BritishPandaChick in Canton, Ohio. She consults with small business, creatives, and solopreneurs to build WordPress websites. Before she was composing web applications, she taught academic skills to students and created instructional materials differentiated for individual student learning needs.

In addition to producing websites, Sarah writes posts for her blogs BritishPandaChick Codes and Needlework Kits. She has contributed her posts to other platforms such as The Practical Dev, App Academy, and Book Prescriber. She has been featuredSarah currently serves as the moderator for Elphas Can Code community on Elpha.

Current Blogs

BritishPandaChick Codes

BritishPandaChick Codes was launched November 2018. All the posts from the My Coding Journey series as well as ones written about carers and books on The Original BritishPandaChick were moved to this new platform. New content soon started appearing March 2019. I introduced new series including 100 Days of Code challenge and CNC2018 Get a Job Challenge.

Needlework Kits

Needlework Kits was launched September 2017. I review needlecraft kits I’ve made. All the craft kits featured on The Original BritishPandaChick were moved to this new platform. New craft kit reviews began appearing in 2018. I collaborated with Plaid Crafts on a couple of posts where I gave feedback on specific craft kits.

The Original BritishPandaChick

The Original BritishPandaChick blog was launched March 2015. I wrote weekly posts on topics that interested me. In June 2016, the My Coding Journey series was introduced. The series was aimed to review and share everything that I’ve learned as a developer. The Original BritishPandaChick Code blog ended October 2018 with many of the posts moved to Needlework Kits and BritishPandaChick Codes.

Disney Codes Challenge

Disney Codes Challenge was launched July 2019. Disney Codes is a 57-day coding challenge. Developers are committed to building web projects inspired by Disney properties each day for 23 minutes. Developers document their progress the challenge with the journal from the challenge repository on Github.

Elphas Can Code

Elphas Can Code was created on the Elpha platform in September 2019. Elphas Can Code is a supportive online community for all Elpha members learning how to code. The community strives to help all members at different stages of their individual coding journeys. I serve as the community moderator where I review posts, enforce Elpha guidelines, and create conversations so all members can contribute.
